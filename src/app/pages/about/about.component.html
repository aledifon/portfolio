About me

Hi! I am Alejandro Díaz Fontalva, a Software Engineer with a background in Industrial Engineering and a strong interest in building software systems. I also studied Electronic Engineering, which strengthened my curiosity about technology and digital systems.

Over the past 8 years, I've worked across different industrial and technological environments, from microelectronics and industrial automation systems (including PLC-based solutions) to desktop software development using .NET and C#. A key part of this journey includes my experience at CERN, where I worked in a highly technical and international environment focused on complex engineering systems. Along the way, I also explored interactive development with Unity, focusing on real-time visualization and user experiences. More recently, I've been expanding my skill set toward frontend development with Angular and modern web technologies, continuously adapting to new tools and environments as I evolve as a developer.

What drives me most is the process of turning ideas into functional and scalable software solutions. I see software development as a craft — a way to build, experiment, and bring ideas to life in meaningful ways.

Outside of work, I enjoy video games and game development, especially for the sense of immersion and creativity they offer. I also like traveling and discovering new cultures, and listening to electronic music.
